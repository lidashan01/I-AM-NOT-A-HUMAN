FEMA Notice — Testing Demands and Safe Responses

When and How FEMA Appears

A FEMA worker can knock during night cycles and demand that a random guest be taken away temporarily for testing. You may have limited room to negotiate; plan headcount accordingly.

Who Gets Taken and What Happens

Selections are not always predictable. Losing a key guest can reduce daytime examination options and weaken night safety. Keep at least one trusted human to avoid loneliness‑based failures.

Planning Tips to Minimize Risk

  • Vet a small core of reliable humans early; keep backups.
  • Budget energy to re‑examine edge cases before FEMA nights.
  • Do not allow suspected Visitors to linger during FEMA windows.

Ending Impact

Endings depend on two conditions: no Visitors left and at least one human present. FEMA events interact with both, so plan for redundancy in human occupants.
