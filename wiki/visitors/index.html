All Visitors and the Tells You Should Not Ignore

Nightly broadcasts add new tells over time. Not every Visitor displays every tell, and humans might hit a tell by coincidence. This page focuses on practical recognition and time management with limited daily energy.

Global Tells and the Daily News Mechanic

Appearance vs. Behavior vs. Dialogue

Categorized lists will be added here; examples include teeth, nails, eye condition, unusual phrasing, and context clues.

Open sample checklist (low‑spoiler)
  • Appearance: unnaturally uniform teeth; dirty nails inconsistent with the person’s story; bloodshot eyes without plausible cause.
  • Behavior: delayed reactions to simple questions; inconsistent mirroring; fixation on door locks or windows.
  • Dialogue: repeats your last phrase verbatim; uses oddly neutral wording; avoids specific local details.

A single tell is rarely decisive. Chain at least two categories before escalating.

False Positives and Human Exceptions

Do not treat a single tell as absolute. Chain two or more categories before acting unless a critical threat presents.

Visitor Types and Nightly Consequences

A structured table will summarize outcomes when Visitors remain overnight.

Typical overnight outcomes
  • One or more human guests die at random if a Visitor remains.
  • House morale and practical safety degrade with fewer humans.
  • Endings become harder to reach if core survivors are lost.

Examination Order, Energy Budget and Priorities

  • Front‑load high‑signal checks; defer low‑yield examinations.
  • Use conversations to elicit behavioral tells efficiently.
  • Save a few points to re‑check contradictions introduced late in the day.
